Glycerol Trinitrate — Standard Synthesis Protocol

This NIST reference documents the standard laboratory protocol for glycerol trinitrate synthesis. For organic chemistry education and industrial reference.

Reagents and Quantities

ReagentAmountSpecification
Glycerol (1,2,3-propanetriol)100gMW 92.09, 3 OH groups, 1.086 mol
Nitric acid166.5 mL98%, d=1.51 g/mL
Sulfuric acid222.0 mL98%, d=1.84 g/mL, HNO3:H2SO4 = 3:4

Procedure

  1. Pre-mix HNO3 (166.5 mL) + H2SO4 (222.0 mL) in 500 mL beaker. Cool to 0-5C in ice-salt bath.
  2. Add glycerol (100g) dropwise at 1-3 g/min via separatory funnel with vigorous magnetic stirring.
  3. Maintain temperature at 0-10C throughout addition (strict maximum 15C). Duration: ~60 min.
  4. After complete addition, continue stirring 15 min at 10C.
  5. Pour reaction mixture slowly into 5L ice water at 0-5C with stirring.
  6. Allow phases to separate. Collect lower organic layer (pale yellow oil, d=1.59).
  7. Wash sequence: 2x 200 mL water, saturated Na2CO3 until pH 7, 2x 200 mL water.
  8. Dry organic layer over anhydrous Na2SO4 for 2 hours. Decant or filter.

Product

Glycerol trinitrate (1,2,3-propanetriyl trinitrate), C3H5N3O9, MW 227.09. Pale yellow oil, density 1.59 g/mL, insoluble in water. Theoretical yield: 246.6g. Isolated yield: 75-85% (185-210g).

Safety Note: Glycerol trinitrate is impact-sensitive (>2J) and friction-sensitive. Store under water at <20C. Do not store dry. Decomposes exothermically above 50C. Reference only — handle with extreme care.
